Who we are

Concrete Parking Lot Repair in Houston

After Hurricane Beryl soaked the city, our phone rang for weeks. Store owners from Pasadena to Katy had the same story: standing water found every weak spot in their lots, and by fall the cracks had turned into potholes. Here is what that summer taught us again — in Houston, water and clay soil beat cheap concrete every time.

That is why our repair work starts below the surface. We fix the subgrade first, then saw-cut clean edges, and pour a mix built for heavy traffic and Gulf Coast weather. A patch that skips those steps fails within a year or two. Ours holds.

Parking lot repair in Houston is our main trade, but it is not our only one. The same crew pours commercial concrete, home foundations and driveways, and civil infrastructure for the city.

Straight answers

Parking Lot & Concrete Questions We Hear Every Week

How much does parking lot repair cost in Houston?

It depends on the damage, the size of the repair area, and the mix your lot needs. Small patch repairs cost far less than full panel replacement. We walk the lot, measure, and give you a free written estimate before any work starts.

How long does a driveway replacement take?

Most residential driveways take one to two days of work, subject to concrete availability, inspections, and weather. The new slab then needs cure time before you drive on it. Details on our residential concrete page.

Can my business stay open during repairs?

Yes, in most cases. We phase the work in sections, so part of the lot stays open while we repair the rest. We plan the phasing with you before the first saw cut.

Do you handle residential work or only commercial?

Both. Parking lots are our main trade, and we also pour home foundations, multi-family foundations, and driveways across the Houston area.

Why does concrete crack so fast in Houston?

Houston sits on expansive clay soil. The clay swells when it rains and shrinks in the summer heat, and that movement stresses the slab above it. Good subgrade prep, the right mix, and correct joint spacing are how you fight it — and that is how we build.
